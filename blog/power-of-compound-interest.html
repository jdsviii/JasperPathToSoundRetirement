The Magic of Compound Interest — Your Retirement’s Best Friend

By Jasper Saunders • Educational content only

Albert Einstein reportedly called compound interest the “eighth wonder of the world.” In retirement planning, it truly is the most powerful force you control. Small, consistent contributions early in life can grow into life-changing sums.

How Compound Interest Works in Practice

Using the growth projection in this calculator, you can see how a $500 monthly contribution at a reasonable 7–8% average return can turn into hundreds of thousands (or millions) over 30–40 years. Time is the variable that matters most.

Starting at age 25 vs age 45 makes an enormous difference. The earlier you start, the more your money works for you.

Key Takeaways for Your Path

  • Start as early as possible — even small amounts matter.
  • Stay consistent through market ups and downs.
  • Use low-cost index funds to maximize the compounding effect.
